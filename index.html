Transformers as dynamics models   Predictive dynamics models often have excellent single-step prediction error, but poor long-horizon accuracy due to compounding errors. We show that transformers are more substantially more reliable long-horizon predictors than state-of-the-art single-step models, even in continuous Markovian domains.




Imitation, goal-reaching, and offline RL   Various control settings can be reduced to slight modifications of beam search with a sequence model.

  • Decoding with standard beam search is an imitative method.
  • Conditioning on a future desired state alongside previously-encountered states gives rise to a goal-reaching method.
  • Replacing log-probabilities from the sequence model reward predictions recovers a model-based planning method. Despite having none of the usual tricks employed to make planning effective with learned models, such as ensembles or distributional penalties, we find this approach surprisingly effective.
